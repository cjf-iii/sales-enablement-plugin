# Proposal Template — SCRAP Framework

## Instructions

Fill in each section following the guidance in brackets. Delete all instructional
text before sending. Refer to `skills/proposal-craft/SKILL.md` for detailed
guidance on each section.

---

# [Your Company Name] — Proposal for [Client Company Name]

**Prepared for:** [Primary contact name, title]
**Prepared by:** [Your name, title]
**Date:** [Date]
**Proposal Valid Through:** [Date — typically 30 days]

---

## Executive Summary

[One page maximum. This should be readable in 90 seconds and contain
the complete argument for why the buyer should invest. Include:
- Their situation in 1-2 sentences
- The cost of the status quo in 1 sentence with a dollar figure
- Your proposed solution in 2-3 sentences focused on outcomes
- ROI headline number
- Clear next step with a specific date]

---

## Situation

[10% of proposal length. Prove you understand their world.

Start with something only YOU could write because you've done the discovery.
Reference their strategic priorities, industry position, and the specific
challenge that brought you together.

Do NOT start with your company history.
Do NOT use generic industry statistics.
Every sentence must connect to THEIR situation.]

---

## Complication

[15% of proposal length. Articulate the cost of the status quo.

Structure as 2-3 compounding dynamics:
1. [Pain point 1] — quantified impact, trend direction
2. [Pain point 2] — quantified impact, how it relates to pain 1
3. [Pain point 3] — competitive or strategic consequence

Include the Cost of Inaction calculation:
"At current trajectory, [specific consequence] represents a
$[X] impact over the next [time period]."]

---

## Resolution

[25% of proposal length. Present your solution as the bridge from
complication to desired outcome.

For each complication identified above, describe:
- The specific outcome your solution delivers
- How it works (high-level, not technical deep-dive)
- Expected timeline to see results
- A proof point or benchmark from a comparable customer

Structure as outcome blocks, NOT feature lists:
- "[Outcome 1]: [Target metric improvement]"
- "[Outcome 2]: [Target metric improvement]"
- "[Outcome 3]: [Target metric improvement]"]

---
