# MEDDICC Framework — Extended MEDDIC

## Overview

MEDDICC extends the classic MEDDIC framework by adding two critical dimensions: **Competition** and **Champion Coaching**. While MEDDIC tells you if a deal is qualified, MEDDICC tells you if a deal is qualified AND positioned to win against alternatives.

**When to use MEDDICC over MEDDIC:** Any deal where a competitor is actively involved, where the buyer is evaluating multiple solutions, or where the incumbent advantage is significant. In media and advertising sales, this means most enterprise deals — there's almost always a competitor or an incumbent.

---

## The Two Additional Dimensions

### C — Competition

**Definition:** A thorough understanding of who you're competing against, how you're positioned relative to them, and a specific strategy to win.

**Why it matters:** Deals aren't won in a vacuum. You're always competing against something — a direct competitor, the status quo ("do nothing"), an internal build option, or a different priority for the same budget. Understanding the competitive landscape determines your positioning, messaging, and trap question strategy.

**What to assess:**

| Factor | Questions to Answer |
|---|---|
| **Who** | Which specific competitors are in the deal? How many? |
| **Stage** | How far along is each competitor? Who got there first? |
| **Relationship** | Does a competitor have existing relationships or an incumbent advantage? |
| **Positioning** | How is the buyer comparing solutions? What criteria favor us vs. them? |
| **Intelligence** | What has the buyer told us about the competitor? What haven't they told us? |
| **Strategy** | What is our specific plan to differentiate and win? |

**Scoring guide:**

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1-2 | Don't know who competitors are, or haven't assessed competitive position |
| 3-4 | Know the competitors but don't have a differentiation strategy |
| 5-6 | Understand competitive landscape and have general positioning |
| 7-8 | Clear differentiation strategy, trap questions prepared, buyer acknowledges our advantages |
| 9-10 | Buyer has explicitly stated preference for us, or we've successfully neutralized all competitive threats |

**Competitive response playbook:**

| Scenario | Strategy |
|---|---|
| No competition | Move fast. Create urgency around the problem, not the evaluation. Lock in before competition emerges. |
| 1 competitor, known | Execute battle card strategy. Focus on differentiation through fit, not features. |
| 2+ competitors | Focus on shaping decision criteria early. The vendor who defines how solutions are evaluated usually wins. |
| Incumbent advantage | Land-and-expand. Don't try to replace everything — win a wedge and prove value. |
| "Build vs. Buy" | Quantify total cost of building (engineering time, maintenance, opportunity cost). Make "build" feel expensive and slow. |
| "Do nothing" (status quo) | This is the most common competitor. Quantify the cost of inaction aggressively. |

### C — Champion Coaching

**Definition:** The degree to which you've actively coached your champion on how to sell internally, handle objections from colleagues, and navigate political dynamics.

**Why it matters:** Having a champion is necessary but not sufficient. A champion who doesn't know how to sell internally is a champion who will fail. Coaching transforms a willing advocate into an effective one.

**What champion coaching looks like:**

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Level 0: No coaching** | Champion is left to figure it out on their own |
| **Level 1: Information sharing** | You've given the champion materials (deck, one-pager) but no guidance on how to use them |
| **Level 2: Talking points** | You've coached the champion on key messages and anticipated questions |
| **Level 3: Objection prep** | You've role-played likely objections with the champion and prepared responses |
| **Level 4: Full coaching** | You've mapped the internal political landscape with the champion, identified allies and blockers, created a stakeholder-specific communication plan, and rehearsed the internal pitch |

**Scoring guide:**

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1-2 | No coaching. Champion has materials but no guidance. |
| 3-4 | Basic coaching. Champion knows the key messages but hasn't practiced objection handling. |
| 5-6 | Moderate coaching. Champion can articulate value and handle basic objections. Has a plan for internal selling. |
| 7-8 | Strong coaching. Champion has been through objection role-play, knows the political landscape, and has a stakeholder-by-stakeholder plan. |
| 9-10 | Expert coaching. Champion is operating like a member of your sales team. They proactively share intelligence, adjust strategy based on internal feedback, and drive the process without your prompting. |

**Champion coaching checklist:**

- [ ] Shared a 1-page value summary tailored for internal presentation
- [ ] Identified the top 3 objections they'll face internally and prepared responses
- [ ] Mapped every stakeholder: who supports, who's neutral, who opposes, and why
- [ ] Created a specific strategy for the economic buyer conversation
- [ ] Role-played the internal pitch with the champion
- [ ] Established a regular check-in cadence for progress updates
- [ ] Given the champion "ammunition" — data, case studies, ROI models — formatted for their internal audience
- [ ] Discussed what personal success looks like for the champion if this deal closes

## MEDDICC vs. MEDDIC — When to Use Which

| Situation | Use |
|---|---|
| Single-vendor evaluation, no competition | MEDDIC is sufficient |
| Competitive deal with 1+ known competitors | MEDDICC — Competition dimension is critical |
| Complex enterprise deal with political dynamics | MEDDICC — Champion Coaching dimension is critical |
| Deal above $250K with 6+ month cycle | MEDDICC — full rigor is warranted |
| Mid-market deal, shorter cycle | MEDDIC is sufficient (or BANT for initial qualification) |

## MEDDICC Scoring Summary

| Dimension | Weight |
|---|---|
| Metrics | 12% |
| Economic Buyer | 16% |
| Decision Criteria | 12% |
| Decision Process | 12% |
| Identify Pain | 16% |
| Champion | 12% |
| Competition | 10% |
| Champion Coaching | 10% |

**Score interpretation (out of 80):**
- **0-20:** Unqualified — significant gaps across multiple dimensions
- **21-40:** Early qualification — opportunity exists but needs substantial work
- **41-56:** Qualified — real deal with manageable gaps
- **57-68:** Well-qualified — strong position with minor refinements needed
- **69-80:** Exceptional — all dimensions validated, high probability of close
