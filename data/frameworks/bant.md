# BANT Framework — Modern Adaptation

## Overview

BANT (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline) is the oldest sales qualification framework, developed at IBM in the 1960s. While simpler than MEDDIC, it remains useful for initial qualification — especially for transactional or mid-market deals where a lighter-weight framework is appropriate.

**When to use BANT:** Deals under $50K, sales cycles under 30 days, or as a first-pass qualifier before applying MEDDIC to complex deals.

**Modern adaptation:** The original BANT assumed buyers follow a linear path (budget → authority → need → timeline). Modern buyers often start with Need, then seek Authority, then establish Timeline, and only then discuss Budget. The framework below reflects this reality.

---

## The Four Dimensions (Modern Order)

### N — Need (Start Here)

**Original BANT:** "Do they need our product?"
**Modern adaptation:** "Do they have a problem that our solution uniquely solves, and is it painful enough to drive action?"

**Qualification bar:**
- GREEN: They've articulated a specific problem with business impact, and they're actively seeking solutions
- YELLOW: They acknowledge a problem but aren't actively looking to solve it ("nice to have")
- RED: No identified need, or their need doesn't align with your solution's strengths

**Key questions:**
1. "What's driving you to look at solutions in this space right now?"
2. "How is this problem affecting your team or business results today?"
3. "What happens if you don't address this in the next 6 months?"

### A — Authority

**Original BANT:** "Can this person buy?"
**Modern adaptation:** "Do you understand the full buying group — who decides, who influences, who blocks — and do you have access to the decision-maker?"

**Qualification bar:**
- GREEN: Decision-maker identified and engaged, buying process is understood
- YELLOW: Decision-maker identified but not yet engaged; buying process partially understood
- RED: Unknown who decides, or your contact has no path to the decision-maker

**Key questions:**
1. "Who else needs to be involved in this decision?"
2. "Have you made a purchase like this before? What was the approval process?"
3. "If you decided this was the right solution, what would need to happen next?"

### T — Timeline

**Original BANT:** "When will they buy?"
**Modern adaptation:** "Is there a compelling event or deadline that creates urgency, or is this an open-ended evaluation?"

**Qualification bar:**
- GREEN: Specific deadline or compelling event driving the timeline (contract expiration, board mandate, seasonal business need)
- YELLOW: General timeframe ("sometime this quarter") without a hard deadline
- RED: "No rush" or "we're just exploring" with no identifiable trigger

**Key questions:**
1. "Is there a specific date or event driving your timeline?"
2. "When would you need a solution live to impact this quarter's results?"
3. "What other initiatives are competing for your team's attention right now?"

### B — Budget

**Original BANT:** "Do they have money?"
**Modern adaptation:** "Is there allocated budget, can budget be created, and does our price point align with their value perception?"

**Qualification bar:**
- GREEN: Budget is allocated or the buyer has a clear path to secure it. Your price range is within their expectations.
- YELLOW: No allocated budget but the buyer has budget authority or a history of creating budget for high-priority initiatives
- RED: No budget, no path to budget, or your price point is fundamentally misaligned with their expectations

**Key questions:**
1. "Has budget been allocated for this initiative, or is that part of the process?"
2. "What range of investment are you anticipating?"
3. "How does your organization typically handle investments that weren't in the original budget plan?"

## BANT-to-MEDDIC Bridge

For deals that pass BANT qualification and warrant deeper analysis:

| BANT | Maps to MEDDIC | What to Dig Deeper On |
|---|---|---|
| Need | Identify Pain + Metrics | Quantify the pain. Get specific numbers. |
| Authority | Economic Buyer + Champion | Separate the EB from the champion. Map the full buying group. |
| Timeline | Decision Process | Map every step between now and signed contract. |
| Budget | Metrics (ROI justification) | Build the financial case that justifies the investment. |

## Scoring

| Dimension | Score 1 | Score 2 | Score 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Need | No identified need | Acknowledged but not urgent | Active, painful, quantified |
| Authority | Unknown decision-maker | Identified, not engaged | Engaged and accessible |
| Timeline | No timeline | General timeframe | Specific deadline |
| Budget | No budget or path | Possible but not allocated | Allocated or easily created |

**Total score 10-12:** Qualified — proceed with full sales process
**Total score 7-9:** Conditionally qualified — address gaps before investing heavily
**Total score 4-6:** Not qualified — nurture or disqualify
