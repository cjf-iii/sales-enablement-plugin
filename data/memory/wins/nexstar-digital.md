# Win Analysis: Nexstar Digital — $520,000

## Deal Summary

Nexstar Digital, the digital arm of Nexstar Media Group (largest local TV station owner in the US), selected our platform for their programmatic yield optimization and audience data activation across 120+ local news sites. The deal was a competitive displacement of FreeWheel, who had been their ad server and yield management platform for 3 years.

## What We Were Competing Against

- **FreeWheel (Comcast):** Incumbent. Deep integration with their ad server. Relationship at CTO level. Pricing advantage through existing MSA.
- **No decision / status quo:** The "keep FreeWheel and do nothing" option was the real competition for the first 6 weeks of the sales cycle.

## Why We Won

### Stated Reason (from the buyer)
"Your managed optimization service will pay for itself in the first quarter. FreeWheel gives us a platform but expects us to run it ourselves — and we don't have the team for that."

### Actual Reason (our assessment)
Three factors, in order of importance:

1. **Managed service vs. self-serve:** Nexstar's ad ops team is lean (6 people across 120+ sites). FreeWheel's platform required 2+ dedicated operators that Nexstar didn't have. Our managed service model removed that burden.

2. **Time-to-value:** We committed to first campaign live within 45 days. FreeWheel's re-integration timeline was 90+ days. The VP of Digital had a Q4 revenue target that required immediate action.

3. **Yield improvement guarantee:** We offered a 20% yield improvement guarantee in the first 90 days, with a clawback if we didn't deliver. FreeWheel wouldn't match this.

## Key Lessons

1. **Managed service is a differentiator for lean teams.** When the buyer can't add headcount, "we do the work" beats "we give you the tools."
2. **Performance guarantees close deals.** The 20% yield improvement guarantee was the moment the SVP went from "interested" to "let's do this."
3. **Incumbents are vulnerable on speed.** FreeWheel's re-integration timeline was 2x ours. When there's a deadline, speed wins.
4. **Champion coaching paid off.** We spent 45 minutes role-playing the internal pitch with the VP of Ad Ops. He later told us that rehearsal was "the reason the SVP approved it in one meeting."
